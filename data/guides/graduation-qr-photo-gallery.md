## The whole graduation, from every angle

Graduation happens fast, and it happens from a distance — you're in the audience while the moment is on stage, and the best candids are on your classmates' phones. A QR code photo gallery closes the gap. One printed code, and family, friends and the whole class upload their photos and videos into a single shared gallery: the walk across the stage, the cap toss, the hugs afterward, the family dinner that evening.

There's no app to install and no account to make — it opens in the browser — so grandparents and classmates alike can join in seconds.

## Why a shared gallery works for graduation

- **Every angle in one place.** Audience shots, stage moments and after-party candids, together.
- **No app, no login.** Everyone can contribute, whatever phone they carry.
- **Original quality.** Full-resolution files, good enough to print or frame the best ones.
- **Private to your circle.** Only people with the link or code can see the gallery.

## Set it up in minutes

1. **Create the gallery.** [Start here](%LP%/create) with the graduate's name and date; the QR code is ready instantly.
2. **Share the code two ways.** Print it for the party tables and send the link to family who join remotely.
3. **Ask people to upload.** A note in the invite and a sign at the dinner is all it takes.
4. **Download and keep it.** Save every original as a ZIP after the day.
