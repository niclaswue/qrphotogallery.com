## One code, every prom photo in one place

Prom generates hundreds of photos across hundreds of phones — and normally they vanish into private accounts and group chats you'll never fully see. A QR code photo gallery fixes that: one printed code, one link, and every student's photos and videos land in a single shared gallery. The candids your official photos miss, the getting-ready shots, the dance floor at 11pm — all of it, in one place.

Because it opens in the browser, there's nothing to install. Point the camera at the code, tap upload, done. That zero-friction step is why a shared gallery fills up when a hashtag doesn't.

## Why a shared gallery beats a hashtag or group chat

- **Everyone can join.** No app and no login means every student takes part, not just the ones on the same platform.
- **Original quality.** Group chats and social apps compress everything. A dedicated gallery keeps every photo and video sharp enough to print.
- **It stays in the year group.** The gallery is private to people with the link — not public, not searchable, not fed to strangers.
- **One download for the organizer.** The teacher, committee or parent running it gets every file in a single ZIP.

## How to set it up for prom

1. **Create the gallery.** [Start here](%LP%/create) with your school and year, and the QR code is ready instantly.
2. **Print the code for the venue.** On table cards, the photo backdrop, and a sign at the entrance. One line — "Scan to share tonight's photos" — is enough.
3. **Announce it once.** A quick word from the host or a slide on the screen sends uploads soaring.
4. **Download everything.** After the night, the organizer saves every original as a ZIP.
