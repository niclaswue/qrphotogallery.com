## What is a QR code wedding photo album?

A QR code wedding photo album is a single online album that your guests fill themselves. You place a printed QR code on the tables, at the entrance or on the menu; guests scan it with their phone camera, and the upload page opens in their browser. Every photo and video they choose lands in one shared album — the candid dance-floor moments, the getting-ready shots, the speeches your official photographer missed.

It replaces the messy mix of "text me your photos later" and a wedding hashtag nobody remembers. Instead of chasing 80 people across five chat apps, you get one link, one album, one download.

## Why a QR code album beats a hashtag or shared drive

- **No app, no login.** Every extra step loses guests. Scanning a code and tapping "upload" is something even the least technical relative can do.
- **Original quality.** Social apps and messengers crush your photos. A dedicated album keeps every file at full resolution, so the album is good enough to print.
- **Everything in one place.** Photos and videos from every phone collect in a single gallery instead of scattered across inboxes.
- **You own it.** At the end of the night you download the whole album — no one's account, no expiring story.

## How to set up your wedding photo album in minutes

1. **Create your album.** [Start your gallery](%LP%/create), give it your names and wedding date, and you're done — your QR code is generated instantly.
2. **Print the QR code.** Add it to table cards, the welcome sign, or the order of service. A short line like "Scan to add your photos" is all the instruction guests need.
3. **Let the album fill up.** Guests scan, upload and keep dancing. New photos appear in the shared gallery in real time.
4. **Download and keep it.** After the wedding, download the entire album as a ZIP and save it wherever you like.

## What your guests see when they scan

When a guest scans the code, they get a clean upload page branded with your names — not a confusing form. They pick photos and videos from their camera roll, tap upload, and can browse everything the album already holds. It works the same on iPhone and Android because it's just a web page.
