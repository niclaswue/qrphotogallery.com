## What is a QR code wedding photo gallery?

A QR code wedding photo gallery is one shared, live gallery that guests reach by scanning a printed code. Every photo and video they upload appears on the same wall, so the whole room is building one gallery together as the night goes on. Your professional photographer captures the posed, planned shots; the gallery captures everything else — the table laughs, the sparkler exit, the toddler asleep under a chair.

Because it's a web page, not an app, the barrier is almost zero: point the camera, tap the notification, upload. That's the difference between a gallery with 30 photos and one with 900.

## Album or gallery — which do you need?

Both start from the same QR code and the same effortless upload. The difference is emphasis:

- A **[wedding photo album](%LP%/guides/wedding-qr-photo-album)** is about the keepsake — collecting every file to browse and download later.
- A **wedding photo gallery** is about the live moment — guests scroll the shared wall during the reception and see the day through each other's eyes.

You don't have to choose in practice: the same gallery does both. Pick the framing that matches how you want guests to experience it.

## How to set up your wedding photo gallery

1. **Create the gallery.** [Start here](%LP%/create) with your names and date; your QR code is ready instantly.
2. **Put the code where guests will see it.** Table numbers, the bar, the photo booth, the welcome sign. One line — "Scan to see and share our photos" — is enough.
3. **Watch it fill up live.** Uploads appear in the shared gallery in real time, so guests keep coming back to see the latest.
4. **Save everything.** Download the full-resolution gallery as a ZIP whenever you like.

## Why couples choose a shared gallery over a hashtag

A wedding hashtag scatters your photos across private accounts, buried under filters and compression, half of them never public. A QR code gallery pulls everything into one place at full quality, viewable by everyone you invite and no one you don't. There's no algorithm, no ads, and nothing expires at midnight.

It also works for the guests who aren't on social media at all — grandparents, colleagues, the friend who deleted Instagram. Everyone gets the same link and the same simple upload.

## Keep the gallery going after the day

The gallery doesn't close when the band stops. It stays online for a year, so guests can add the photos they took ages to sort through, and you can share the link with anyone who missed the celebration. When you're ready, the host dashboard downloads every original in one ZIP and lets you remove anything you'd rather not keep.
